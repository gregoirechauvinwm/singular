Backed by science
01

Attachment theory & longitudinal data

Drawing from Bowlby, Hazan & Shaver, and 40 years of attachment research, we model relational styles and predict long-term dyadic stability with validated psychometric instruments.

02

Neurobiological compatibility markers

We examine HLA-mediated immune diversity, oxytocin receptor polymorphisms, and cortisol synchrony — biological signals that influence pair bonding beyond conscious preference.

03

Bayesian compatibility modelling

Our matching algorithm is a probabilistic model, not a rule-set. It updates continuously from participant outcomes, treating each long-term relationship as new evidence.

04

Longitudinal outcome tracking

Unlike survey-based studies, we track matched pairs at 3, 12, 36, and 60 months — measuring relationship quality, conflict resolution, and sustained satisfaction.

Project phases
Phase I
2024 – 2025
Complete

Foundation & instrument validation

Development and validation of the Singular Compatibility Battery — a 220-item assessment covering attachment, values, cognitive style, biological markers, and life trajectory. Validated across 1,800 participants in 12 countries.

Phase II
2025 – 2026
Active

Matching trials & algorithm refinement

Controlled matching trials with 4,200 enrolled participants. Pairs are matched algorithmically and followed prospectively. Real-world outcome data is fed back into the Bayesian model in quarterly update cycles.

Phase III
2027 – 2029
Forthcoming

Open protocol & global deployment

Publication of the full matching protocol as an open standard. Partnerships with research institutions and clinical practices worldwide. Our goal: make rigorous, science-based partnership matching universally accessible.
